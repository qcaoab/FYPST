What you can do

On this website, you can explore the magic of Neural Style Transfer, view and generate your own artworks in the following pages:

  • Gallery

    In page "Gallery", there are two sections of artwork samples.

    In section one, you can view some pre-defined artistic styles to which user inputs can be transferred, such the style of Van Gogh, Monet, Japanese ukiyoe, and so on.

    In section two, there are three kinds of pictures in each row: the input content, the input style, and the output result.

    To explore more details about the gallery, nevigate to "Gallery" from menu or click here.

  • Create-1

    It's easy to transfer your own picture to one of our provided styles. What you need to do is to choose your photo and select one of the style names from the dropdown. Click the upload button and you can find the result below.

    To try it yourself, nevigate to "Create1" from menu or click here.

  • Create-2

    You can also transfer your picture to arbitrary styles of your choice!

    By uploading a photo-like content picture and a painting-like style reference picture, you can get an output picture with the same content painted in the style you uploaded.

    To try it yourself, nevigate to "Create2" from menu or click here.

The principles behind the functionalities on this website (for your interest)

The first functionality "Create1" is implemented according to the CycleGAN model, which was proposed by Jun-Yan Zhu and his colleagues [1]. It trains on unpaired images from two source domains. Not like some other inconvenient neural style transfer algorithm, the advantage is that it is very flexible and avoids the requirement of paired images as training data.

As illustrated in the above figure [1], X and Y are two domains, with two mapping G: from X to Y and F: from Y to X. Dx and Dy are two adversarial discriminators, where Dx can discriminate image x from translated image F(y) and Dy can do the inverse. Two directions of mapping can be trained at the same time. The final trained model can then be used to generate the style transferred image by passing the original image through the network.

[1] is referenced from https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.10593.

The second functionality "Create2" is implemented according to the CNN-based AdaIN model, which was proposed by Xun Huang and Serge Belongieand [1]. Because it includes manipulations on the VGG-19 convolutional network when transferring styles, it is regarded as a CNN-based algorithm here. As illustrated in the following figure [1], the input content and style images are encoded by the pre-trained VGG network into abstract features. In this process, only up to several ReLU layers in the VGG are used. Then the AdaIN layer transfers the style of the content features by aligning their mean and variance with those of the style features. The decoder, which is an inverted version of the encoder, can be trained to decode the features into the resulting image.

The advantage of this algorithm is that it enables very fast style transfer of arbitrary content and style image pairs. The computational cost of testing is extremely low, and also, there is no need to train separate models for different styles.

[1] is referenced from https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.06868.
