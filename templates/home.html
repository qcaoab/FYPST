Introduction

Since the dawn of human history, human beings have formed the tendency to appreciate beauty. The aesthetic pursuit of human nature inspires the development of art. As a result, innumerable masterpieces of artwork were born in history and were admired by people until now. Nevertheless, the creation of such artworks can never be easy, as it requires not only the professional skills of the artists but also a large amount of time. Therefore, how to create artworks in a more efficient way has aroused the interest of modern researchers. Fortunately, as deep learning techniques developed, a solution with Neural Style Transfer (NST) was provided.

What we can do

In this project, we have implemented a Neural Style Transfer system that can generate new images of artworks in two ways.

  • For the first one, we have built a template art gallery from which you can view some pre-defined artistic styles can be transferred to, such as Van Gogh’s style, Monet’s style, Japanese ukiyoe style, and so on. To explore more details about this, navigate through the top menu bar and click the “GALLERY” button or here. You may also find some interesting demos of style transfer in that section. To transfer your image into one of these pre-defined styles is really simple. What you need to do is uploading your own content image and freely choose from one of these popular styles to generate your transferred image as desired. To explore more details and experience this functionality, please also navigate through the top menu bar and click the “CREATE1” button or here.
  • For the second one, you can upload two images, namely, a photo-like content image and a painting-like style reference image. By performing the learning algorithm, the output image will have the same content as the content image but painted in the style as the style reference image. To explore more details and have a taste of this functionality, navigate through the top menu bar and click the “CREATE2” button or here. You can also view some sample demonstration results in the “GALLERY” section appeared in the menu bar or here.
